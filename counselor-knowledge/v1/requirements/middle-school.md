## 4. Extracurricular Activities (Mandatory)

> **Required by: ALL schools** — Format varies

### 4.1 Activity List Format
Each activity should include:

| Field | Required | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Activity Name | ✅ Mandatory | |
| Category | ✅ Mandatory | Sports, Arts, Academic, Community Service, etc. |
| Organization/Club | ✅ Mandatory | Where the activity takes place |
| Years of Participation | ✅ Mandatory | e.g., "2020-present" or "3 years" |
| Frequency/Time Commitment | ✅ Mandatory | Hours per week, sessions per week |
| Role/Position | Recommended | Participant, Team Member, Leader, Captain |
| Level | Recommended | Recreational, Competitive, Advanced |
| Achievements/Awards | Recommended | Specific accomplishments |
| Why Meaningful | Recommended | Brief statement on personal significance |
| Plan to Continue | Optional | Some schools ask |

### 4.2 Common Activity Categories
- **Athletics/Sports**: Team sports, martial arts, individual sports, dance
- **Performing Arts**: Music (instruments, voice), theater, dance
- **Visual Arts**: Drawing, painting, design, photography
- **Academic Enrichment**: Math programs (RSM, Kumon), coding, robotics, science clubs
- **Community Service/Volunteering**: Service organizations, community involvement
- **Leadership**: Student government, club leadership, team captain roles
- **Cultural Activities**: Cultural dance, language programs, heritage activities
- **Special Interests**: Creative writing, debate, Model UN, etc.

### 4.3 Achievements & Awards
| Field | Required | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Achievement/Award Title | Recommended | |
| Granting Organization | Recommended | |
| Date Received | Recommended | |
| Level of Competition | Recommended | School, Regional, State, National |
| Significance/Story | Recommended | What it means to the student |

## 5. Application Essays & Questions (Varies by School)

> **This is where schools differ most significantly**

### 5.1 Student Questions

#### Mandatory (All Schools)
| Question Type | Example |
|---------------|---------|
| Activity/Interest Description | "List and describe your activities and interests, ranked by importance" |

#### Common Student Questions (Most Schools)
| Question Type | Schools That Require | Example Prompt |
|---------------|---------------------|----------------|
| Why This School | Harker, Nueva, Crystal, Menlo | "Why are you interested in attending [School]?" |
| Free Time/Interests | Nueva, Menlo | "How would you spend 3 hours of free time?" |
| Dream Class | Nueva | "Describe the most interesting class you've ever imagined taking" |
| Personal Object/Significance | Crystal | "Choose an object that has special significance to you" |
| Book/Movie/Show Recommendation | Nueva, Menlo | "What's one book everyone should read and why?" |
| Proud Moment | Menlo | "What's something you're really proud of?" |
| Friendship | Menlo | "What kind of friend are you?" |
| Superpower Question | Menlo | "If you could have any superpower, what would it be?" |
| Excitement About School | Nueva, Menlo | "What excites you about middle school?" |
| Two Wishes | Crystal (option) | "You've been granted two wishes—what would they be?" |

#### School-Specific Student Requirements
| School | Special Requirements |
|--------|---------------------|
| **Nueva** | Creative Project (original work showcasing passions) |
| **Menlo** | Ten Minute Takes (quick, fun questions) |
| **Menlo** | Kira Video Responses (recorded answers) |
| **BASIS** | Minimal essays; entrance exam focused |

### 5.2 Parent Questions

#### Common Parent Questions (Most Schools)
| Question Type | Schools That Require | Example Prompt |
|---------------|---------------------|----------------|
| Why This School | All | "Why is your child interested in attending [School]?" |
| Child at Their Best | Nueva, Crystal, Menlo | "Tell us a story about your child at their best" |
| Areas for Growth | Nueva | "Tell us about an area of challenge and growth" |
| Child's Character | Menlo | "Describe your child's character" |
| Family Values | Nueva | "What values matter most to your family in education?" |
| Additional Context | Nueva, Crystal | "Share anything that helps us know your child" |
| School Involvement | Nueva | "How have you been involved in your child's current school?" |
| Hopes for Middle School | Menlo | "What are your hopes for your child's middle school experience?" |

#### School-Specific Parent Requirements
| School | Special Requirements |
|--------|---------------------|
| **Menlo** | Kira Video Responses (recorded parent answers) |
| **Nueva** | Parent Written Questionnaire (detailed) |

### 5.3 Character/Word Limits
| Limit Type | Typical Range |
|------------|---------------|
| Short Answer | 150-500 characters |
| Standard Essay | 500-1,500 characters |
| Long Essay | 1,500-2,500 characters |

| English/Language Arts Teacher | ✅ Mandatory | Current or recent year |
| Math Teacher | ✅ Mandatory | Current or recent year (some schools) |

### 6.2 Administrative Recommendations

| Recommendation Type | Required | Notes |
|--------------------|----------|-------|
| Principal/Head of School | School-Specific | Required by most schools |
| School Counselor | School-Specific | Required by some schools |

### 6.3 Recommendation Request Best Practices
Each request should include:
- School name and application deadline
- School's values and what they're looking for
- Key observations about the student (helpful starting points)
- Specific examples of character, growth, or achievement
- Thank you and appreciation

| Element | Notes |

## Appendix B: Recommended Preparation Timeline

| Timeframe | Tasks |
|-----------|-------|
| **6+ months before deadline** | Research schools, visit campuses, gather report cards |
| **4-5 months before** | Begin activity list, identify recommenders |
| **3-4 months before** | Request transcripts, start essay drafts |
| **2-3 months before** | Finalize essays, send recommendation requests |
| **1-2 months before** | Complete applications, submit early if possible |
| **Deadline month** | Final review, submit, confirm receipt |
| **Post-submission** | Prepare for interviews, send thank-you notes |

## Appendix C: Essay Writing Tips

### For Students
1. **Be authentic** — Admissions officers read thousands of essays; genuine voice stands out
2. **Show, don't tell** — Use specific examples and stories
3. **Answer the question** — Don't use a generic essay for every school
4. **Mention the school specifically** — Show you've researched them
5. **Proofread** — Have parents and teachers review

### For Parents
1. **Tell stories** — Specific moments are more compelling than general descriptions
2. **Be honest about growth areas** — Shows self-awareness
3. **Connect to school values** — Demonstrate fit
4. **Don't over-polish** — Authentic voice matters
5. **Collaborate with your child** — They should recognize themselves in your description
