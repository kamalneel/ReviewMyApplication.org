## 4. Standardized Testing (Varies by School)

> **This is a key differentiator between schools**

### 4.1 Common Entrance Exams
| Test | Schools That Require | Description |
|------|---------------------|-------------|
| **HSPT (High School Placement Test)** | Catholic schools (Bellarmine, Sacred Heart Prep, Mitty, Notre Dame) | Tests verbal, quantitative, reading, math, language |
| **ISEE (Independent School Entrance Exam)** | Many independent schools | Lower, Middle, Upper levels; tests verbal, quantitative, reading, math, essay |
| **SSAT (Secondary School Admission Test)** | Some independent schools | Middle and Upper levels; tests verbal, quantitative, reading, writing sample |
| **School-Specific Entrance Exam** | Some schools (e.g., BASIS) | Custom assessment designed by the school |

### 4.2 Testing Policies by School Type
| School Type | Testing Policy |
|-------------|----------------|
| **Catholic High Schools** | Generally require HSPT |
| **Non-Sectarian Private Schools** | ISEE or SSAT, or test-optional |
| **Progressive Schools** | Often test-optional or test-free |
| **Competitive Academic Schools** | May require ISEE/SSAT plus school exam |
| **Public Magnet Schools** | May require district assessment |

### 4.3 Test Score Usage
| How Scores Are Used | Notes |
|--------------------|-------|
| Admission Qualification | Meeting minimum threshold |
| Class Placement | Determining course level (honors vs. regular) |
| Scholarship Consideration | Merit-based financial aid |
| Holistic Review Component | One factor among many |

### 4.4 Schools with Test-Optional/Test-Free Policies
> Growing number of schools do NOT require standardized testing:

- The Bay School of San Francisco
- Urban School of San Francisco
- Drew School
- Many progressive independent schools

Each activity should include:

| Field | Required | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Activity Name | ✅ Mandatory | |
| Category | ✅ Mandatory | Athletics, Arts, Academic, Community Service, Leadership |

### 5.2 Common Activity Categories

- Team Sports: Basketball, Soccer, Volleyball, Baseball/Softball, Football, Lacrosse, Water Polo

#### Performing Arts
- Music: Instrumental (piano, violin, guitar, etc.), Vocal, Band, Orchestra, Choir

- Dance: Company, Troupe, Performance Groups

#### Visual Arts
- Drawing, Painting, Sculpture
- Photography, Digital Art, Graphic Design
- Ceramics, Textiles, Mixed Media

#### Academic Enrichment
- Math Enrichment: RSM, Kumon, Math Olympiad, MATHCOUNTS
- Science: Science Olympiad, Robotics, Coding Clubs, Science Fair
- Language Arts: Debate, Speech, Model UN, Creative Writing, Journalism
- Academic Competitions: Spelling Bee, Geography Bee, Quiz Bowl

#### Community Service & Leadership
- Volunteering: Hospitals, Food Banks, Environmental Organizations
- Religious/Faith-Based: Youth Groups, Service Projects
- School Leadership: Student Government, Peer Mentoring, Ambassador Programs
- Community Organizations: Scouts, 4-H, Boys & Girls Club

#### Cultural Activities
- Cultural Dance: Indian Classical (Bharatanatyam, Kathak), Chinese, Mexican Folklorico
- Language Programs: Chinese School, Hindi School, Heritage Language Classes
- Cultural Organizations: Community cultural centers, heritage celebrations

## 6. Application Essays & Questions (Varies by School)

> **Essays are crucial for private school applications**

### 6.1 Common Student Essay Types

#### Personal Statement / "Tell Us About Yourself"
| Example Prompts | Notes |
|-----------------|-------|
| "Tell us about yourself and what makes you unique" | Most common format |
| "What would you want us to know about you that we can't learn from the rest of your application?" | Focus on personality, not achievements |
| "Describe yourself in your own words" | Authenticity valued |

#### "Why This School" Essays
| Example Prompts | Notes |
|-----------------|-------|
| "Why are you interested in [School Name]?" | Must be school-specific |
| "What do you hope to gain from attending [School Name]?" | Connect interests to offerings |
| "How do you see yourself contributing to our community?" | Show fit |

#### Challenge/Growth Essays
| Example Prompts | Notes |
|-----------------|-------|
| "Describe a challenge you've faced and how you handled it" | Shows resilience |
| "Tell us about a time you failed and what you learned" | Growth mindset valued |
| "Describe an experience that changed how you think" | Self-reflection |

#### Interest/Passion Essays
| Example Prompts | Notes |
|-----------------|-------|
| "What are you passionate about and why?" | Show depth of interest |
| "Describe something you could talk about for hours" | Reveals authentic interests |
| "What would you teach in a class for your peers?" | Creative thinking |

#### Character/Values Essays
| Example Prompts | Notes |
|-----------------|-------|
| "What qualities do you value most in yourself?" | Self-awareness |
| "What kind of friend/classmate are you?" | Community fit |
| "Describe a time you stood up for something you believe in" | Integrity |

### 6.2 Common Parent Essay/Questionnaire Topics

| Topic | Notes |
|-------|-------|
| "Describe your child" | Character, personality, strengths |
| "What should we know about your child that isn't elsewhere in the application?" | Unique context |
| "What are your child's strengths and areas for growth?" | Balanced view |
| "Why is [School Name] the right fit for your family?" | Family values alignment |
| "How do you support your child's education?" | Parental involvement |
| "Describe a memorable moment with your child" | Storytelling |
| "What are your hopes for your child's high school experience?" | Goals and expectations |

### 6.3 Essay Length Guidelines
| Essay Type | Typical Length |
|------------|----------------|
| Short Response | 100-250 words |
| Standard Essay | 250-500 words |
| Extended Essay | 500-750 words |
| Character Limit Essays | 500-2,500 characters |

Recommenders are typically asked to evaluate:

| Area | Description |
|------|-------------|

- Provide school information, deadline, and submission method
- Share student's resume/activity list
- Include talking points about qualities to highlight
- Follow up with thank-you notes

## 8. Interviews & Campus Visits (Varies by School)

> **Many schools require or strongly recommend interviews**

### 8.1 Interview Types

| Type | Description | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| **Student Interview** | One-on-one with admissions officer | Most common |
| **Group Interview** | Multiple applicants together | Some schools use |
| **Family Interview** | Parents and student together | Less common |
| **Student Panel Interview** | Interviewed by current students | Some schools include |

### 8.2 Common Interview Formats

| Format | Duration | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| In-Person On-Campus | 20-45 minutes | Preferred by most schools |
| Virtual/Video | 20-30 minutes | Alternative for distance |
| Shadow Day + Interview | Half or full day | Combines visit with interview |

### 8.3 Common Interview Questions

#### About the Student
- "Tell me about yourself"
- "What are you passionate about?"
- "What's your favorite subject and why?"
- "What do you like to do outside of school?"
- "What are your strengths and weaknesses?"
- "Describe a challenge you've overcome"

#### About School Fit
- "Why are you interested in our school?"
- "What programs here excite you?"
- "How would you contribute to our community?"
- "What are you looking for in a high school?"

#### Critical Thinking
- "What book are you reading? Tell me about it"
- "If you could change one thing about your current school, what would it be?"
- "What's something you'd like to learn more about?"

### 8.4 Campus Visits

| Visit Type | Description | Notes |
|------------|-------------|-------|
| **Open House** | General information session | Typically fall/early winter |
| **Campus Tour** | Guided tour of facilities | Often paired with info session |
| **Shadow Day** | Spend day with current student | Best for authentic experience |
| **Class Visit** | Sit in on actual classes | Some schools offer |
| **Special Events** | Sports games, performances, fairs | Shows school culture |

### 8.5 Visit Best Practices
- Attend early in application season (fall)
- Take notes on specific programs, facilities, culture
- Prepare thoughtful questions
- Dress appropriately (business casual)
- Send thank-you notes afterward
- Reference visit experiences in essays/interview

## Appendix C: Essay Writing Tips for High School Applications

### For Students
1. **Be authentically YOU** — Don't try to sound like an adult; your voice should shine through
2. **Tell stories** — Specific moments are more memorable than general statements
3. **Show growth** — Demonstrate learning and development
4. **Be specific about the school** — Generic essays are obvious
5. **Take risks** — Unusual topics can stand out (if done well)
6. **Proofread** — Multiple times, and have others review
7. **Follow word limits** — Respect the constraints given

### For Parents
1. **Let your child's personality shine** — Don't over-edit their voice
2. **Be honest** — Admissions officers read thousands of essays
3. **Share specific stories** — Concrete examples are powerful
4. **Acknowledge growth areas** — Shows self-awareness
5. **Connect to school values** — Demonstrate fit
6. **Don't exaggerate** — Authenticity matters

---

## Appendix D: Interview Preparation Tips

1. **Research the school** — Know programs, values, recent news
2. **Practice common questions** — But don't sound rehearsed
3. **Prepare questions to ask** — Shows genuine interest
4. **Dress appropriately** — Business casual is safe
5. **Be yourself** — Authenticity is valued
6. **Make eye contact** — Shows confidence and engagement
7. **Show enthusiasm** — But keep it genuine
8. **Follow up** — Send a thank-you email within 24 hours
